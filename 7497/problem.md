## Description

<div><p>The start of the new academic year brought about the problem of accommodation students into dormitories. One of such dormitories has a <span class="tex-span"><i>a</i> × <i>b</i></span> square meter wonder room. The caretaker wants to accommodate exactly <span class="tex-span"><i>n</i></span> students there. But the law says that there must be at least 6 square meters per student in a room (that is, the room for <span class="tex-span"><i>n</i></span> students must have the area of at least <span class="tex-span">6<i>n</i></span> square meters). The caretaker can enlarge any (possibly both) side of the room by an arbitrary positive integer of meters. Help him change the room so as all <span class="tex-span"><i>n</i></span> students could live in it and the total area of the room was as small as possible.</p></div><div class="input-specification"><p>The first line contains three space-separated integers <span class="tex-span"><i>n</i></span>, <span class="tex-span"><i>a</i></span> and <span class="tex-span"><i>b</i></span> (<span class="tex-span">1 ≤ <i>n</i>, <i>a</i>, <i>b</i> ≤ 10<sup class="upper-index">9</sup></span>) — the number of students and the sizes of the room.</p></div><div class="output-specification"><p>Print three integers <span class="tex-span"><i>s</i></span>, <span class="tex-span"><i>a</i><sub class="lower-index">1</sub></span> and <span class="tex-span"><i>b</i><sub class="lower-index">1</sub></span> <span class="tex-span">(<i>a</i> ≤ <i>a</i><sub class="lower-index">1</sub>;&nbsp;<i>b</i> ≤ <i>b</i><sub class="lower-index">1</sub>)</span> — the final area of the room and its sizes. If there are multiple optimal solutions, print any of them.</p></div>

## Input

<p>The first line contains three space-separated integers <span class="tex-span"><i>n</i></span>, <span class="tex-span"><i>a</i></span> and <span class="tex-span"><i>b</i></span> (<span class="tex-span">1 ≤ <i>n</i>, <i>a</i>, <i>b</i> ≤ 10<sup class="upper-index">9</sup></span>) — the number of students and the sizes of the room.</p>

## Output

<p>Print three integers <span class="tex-span"><i>s</i></span>, <span class="tex-span"><i>a</i><sub class="lower-index">1</sub></span> and <span class="tex-span"><i>b</i><sub class="lower-index">1</sub></span> <span class="tex-span">(<i>a</i> ≤ <i>a</i><sub class="lower-index">1</sub>;&nbsp;<i>b</i> ≤ <i>b</i><sub class="lower-index">1</sub>)</span> — the final area of the room and its sizes. If there are multiple optimal solutions, print any of them.</p>

3 3 5
